Kinisi: A Platform for Autonomizing Off-Road Vehicles

For my engineering capstone, I worked on autonomizing an off-road baja vehicle. I developed the entire electrical system and all of the embedded software for the vehicle. I developed the communication network between nodes responsible for actuating the vehicle. I also helped develop the decision making algorithms, leveraging data fusion and computer vision to help the vehicle navigate. I also developed some of the mechanical system that gave the embedded system feedback as to where the steering was. The autonomous system can be turned off, and the vehicle retains full human driving operability as well as steering and braking capabilities. The long-term goal of this project, outside of the scope of this team’s one year timeline, was for the baja vehicle to be able to navigate through off road terrain in order to rescue and provide aid to a distressed individual. The project took home first place in the Electrical and Computer Engineering departments judging of capstone projects! You can find the entire report here.

                      

Software Engineering Project

I was part of a ten-person student team working in a class competition to apply Agile development methodologies and software design patterns in Java to create (1) an online alcohol label submission form for manufacturers, (2) a form processing workflow application for US Treasury agents in the Alcohol and Tobacco Tax & Trade Bureau (TTB), and (3) an alcohol beverage search engine for the general public. The software systems we created were to inform the TTB about potential features, user interfaces, and design approaches that they might consider implementing.

As a software engineer, I helped gather software requirements by creating surveys and interviews, and brainstorming with the team, as well as creating user stories, scenarios and storyboards. I was responsible for implementing the "special feature" components of the application, that set our team apart from other teams in the class. I implemented security features, as well as some time-saving features like showing changes (similar to git-diff) to the agents so that they would not have to review the application entirely again. I also implemented some hardware features such as RFID login, and some CV features using the Tesseract OCR to read alcohol labels.

Real Time Tennis Match Prediction using Machine Learning

Being the avid tennis lover that I am, I worked on a team to create a program to predict the outcome of tennis matches before the match using Logistic Regression. It was 74.5% correct out of 2801 matches before the match had even begun. We then updated prediction throughout the match using SVM. The merged algorithms yielded 76% correct predictions before 50% of the match was complete, and was 97.2% correct by the end. This program was able to actually predict some serious upsets in the ATP Nitto Finals tournament! Feel free to view the final report here.

A Greener Venice: The Inventory and Reutilization of Green Spaces on the Giudecca Island

I was lucky enough to do my study abroad in Venice, Italy, where I worked on a team of four to asses the under-utilized land on the Giudecca Island in Italy. Our study identified and classified green spaces on the island, we developed a methodology to classify each of the green spaces as an Urban Wild, Park, Private Garden, or Farmland, and proposed options for reutilization. This methodology can be implemented in other parts of Venice or its Lagoon. If implemented, our recommendations for reutilization on Giudecca would increase usable land by 31%, ultimately making for a more sustainable and greener Venice. In order for other researchers to continue our work, I developed a QGIS plug-in that classified the best way to re-utilize the unused land (at least according to our methodologies).
